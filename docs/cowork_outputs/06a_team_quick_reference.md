## How to Add a New Lead

1. On the main dashboard, look for the **"+ New Lead"** or **"Quick Add"** button (usually in the top right area of the Leads screen).
2. Fill in the required fields:
   - **Lead Name** — first and last name
   - **Phone Number** — 10-digit number
   - **Source** — where the lead came from (Website, Referral, Walk-In, etc.)
   - **Agent** — which agent is handling this lead
3. Fill in any other information you have (email, risk address, coverage interest).
4. Click **Save** or **Submit**.

The lead will appear in the list immediately. If flows are active, the assigned agent will get an automatic email notification.

## How to Update a Lead's Status

1. Find the lead in the dashboard (use the search bar or scroll through the list).
2. Click the lead to open it.
3. Find the **Status** field and change it to the appropriate value:
   - **Working** — you're actively in contact with this person
   - **Quoted** — a quote has been prepared and sent
   - **Bound** — the policy has been sold and bound 🎉
   - **Lost** — the lead did not convert
4. Click **Save**.

> If you mark a lead **Bound** or **Lost**, the system will automatically log the change and send notifications — you don't need to do anything extra.

## How to Send an Email Template

1. Open the lead you want to email.
2. Look for an **"Email"** or **"Send Template"** button on the lead detail screen.
3. Select the template you want to use from the dropdown (e.g., "New Lead Introduction," "Quote Ready," "Follow-Up Reminder").
4. Review the email before sending — check that the merge fields filled in correctly (name, phone, quote amount, etc.).
5. Click **Send**.

> The email will come from the agent's name and the shared inbox. The lead will receive it as a normal email.

---

## How to Log a Phone Call

1. Open the lead record.
2. Find the **"Log Activity"** or **"Add Note"** section.
3. Select **Activity Type: Phone Call** from the dropdown.
4. Add a short note about what was discussed (e.g., "Called, left voicemail" or "Spoke with prospect, interested in home quote, call back Friday").
5. Save.

This keeps the conversation history in one place so anyone on the team can see what's happened with a lead at a glance.

## What the Activity Timeline Shows

When you open a lead, you'll see a timeline or list of past activity on that lead. Each entry shows:

- **What happened** — call logged, email sent, status changed, etc.
- **When it happened** — date and time
- **Who did it** — which agent or the system (for automatic events)

This is your receipts. If there's ever a question about whether someone followed up on a lead, the timeline has the answer. Keep it updated.
